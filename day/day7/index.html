♨️ Day 7: North Iceland

July 20

"The clearest way into the Universe is through a forest wilderness."

- John Muir

Day Summary

Journey across Iceland's diverse northeastern landscapes, from the dramatic sea cliffs of the East Fjords to the geothermal wonders near Lake Mývatn. Today's route takes you through remote countryside to the thundering Dettifoss waterfall, the colorful Viti Crater in the Krafla volcanic area, and the relaxing Mývatn Nature Baths, before reaching Akureyri, the capital of the North. After several days of intense hiking, today offers a perfect balance of scenic driving with shorter walks to spectacular natural wonders.

Total Driving: ~285 km (177 miles), 4 hours 45 minutes
  • Ásgeirsstaðir Cottages to Ker Cliffs: ~60 km (37 miles), 1 hour
  • Ker Cliffs to Dettifoss Waterfall: ~120 km (75 miles), 2 hours
  • Dettifoss Waterfall to Viti Crater: ~105 km (65 miles), 1 hour 15 minutes
  • Viti Crater to Mývatn Nature Baths: ~5 km (3 miles), 10 minutes
  • Mývatn Nature Baths to Goðafoss Waterfall: ~45 km (28 miles), 40 minutes
  • Goðafoss Waterfall to Airbnb in Akureyri: ~30 km (19 miles), 30 minutes

Daily Schedule

07:30

Breakfast at Ásgeirsstaðir Cottages

Enjoy a self-prepared breakfast at your cottage before starting a day of diverse sightseeing.

08:30

Drive to Ker Cliffs (Þerribjörg Sea Cliffs)

Head to this hidden gem of East Iceland offering breathtaking coastal views.

09:30

Ker Cliffs Hike

Explore these dramatic sea cliffs that form one of the most breathtaking coastlines in East Iceland. The easy 1.5-hour family-friendly hike offers spectacular views of the ocean and surrounding landscapes.

11:15

Drive to Dettifoss Waterfall

Journey through the highlands to reach Europe's most powerful waterfall.

13:30

Dettifoss Waterfall

Experience Europe's most powerful waterfall, part of Vatnajökull National Park and a UNESCO World Heritage site. This massive waterfall is 328 feet wide and 148 feet high, creating an impressive display of raw natural power.

15:15

Drive to Viti Crater

Continue your journey to the colorful Viti Crater in the Krafla volcanic area.

16:30

Viti Crater

Explore this striking explosion crater with its vivid blue-green water set in a dramatic volcanic landscape. The name 'Viti' means 'hell' in Icelandic, and it was formed during the massive eruptions of 1724 known as the 'Mývatn Fires.'

17:45

Drive to Mývatn Nature Baths

A short drive to the Lake Mývatn area for a relaxing geothermal experience.

18:00

Mývatn Nature Baths

Relax in these geothermal baths, often called the 'Blue Lagoon of the North'. Enjoy the mineral-rich waters with views of the surrounding volcanic landscape - a perfect way to unwind after several days of hiking and exploration.

19:45

Drive to Goðafoss Waterfall

Continue your journey toward Akureyri, stopping at this historically significant waterfall along the way.

20:30

Goðafoss Waterfall

Visit the 'Waterfall of the Gods,' one of Iceland's most spectacular waterfalls with historical significance. According to the Sagas, in the year 1000, the Lawspeaker Þorgeir Ljósvetningagoði threw his pagan idols into the falls when Iceland converted to Christianity.

21:30

Drive to Akureyri

Complete your journey to Iceland's northern capital.

22:00

Check in at Airbnb in Akureyri

Arrive at your accommodation in Akureyri and settle in for the evening.

Key Highlights of the Day

  • Witnessing the raw power of Europe's mightiest waterfall, Dettifoss
  • Hiking along the dramatic Ker Cliffs with panoramic ocean views
  • Exploring the colorful Viti Crater in the active Krafla volcanic area
  • Relaxing in the mineral-rich waters of Mývatn Nature Baths
  • Experiencing the beauty and history of Goðafoss, the 'Waterfall of the Gods'

Alternative Activities

  • Ásbyrgi Canyon: Horseshoe-shaped canyon with impressive rock formations, lush greenery, and peaceful atmosphere. According to Norse mythology, formed by the hoofprint of Odin's eight-legged horse, Sleipnir.
  • Námaskarð Geothermal Area: Otherworldly landscape with bubbling mud pools, steaming fumaroles, and colorful mineral deposits. The sulfurous, multicolored pass offers an excellent introduction to Iceland's geothermal activity.
  • Krafla Power Station: Visitor center providing information about geothermal energy and how Iceland harnesses this sustainable power source. Offers insight into Iceland's renewable energy production.
  • Grjótagjá Cave: Small lava cave with a hot spring inside (viewing only, no swimming allowed). Famous as a filming location for Game of Thrones, this otherworldly cave features crystal clear blue water.
  • Hverfjall Crater Hike: Dramatic volcanic crater with panoramic views from the rim. The 2-mile loop around the rim takes about 1 hour and offers spectacular views of the Lake Mývatn area.
  • Bergárfoss Waterfall: The prettiest and most secluded waterfall in North Iceland, rarely visited even by locals. From Kolugljúfur Canyon, take gravel road south on eastern river bank for 4km, then walk.💎 Hidden Gem

Bad Weather Alternatives

  • Myvatn Nature Baths: The geothermal baths are actually more atmospheric in light rain or snow, with the steam creating a mystical environment.
  • Sigurgeir's Bird Museum: Impressive collection of stuffed birds from around Iceland, including all Icelandic breeding birds except one. Modern museum building with lake views.
  • Akureyri Art Museum: Contemporary art museum housed in a former dairy cooperative building. Features rotating exhibitions of Icelandic and international artists.

While the Mývatn area is famous for its natural beauty, it's also notorious for midges (small flies) in summer. Though not harmful, they can be annoying - consider bringing a head net if you're particularly sensitive.

Notes

  • This is a day with significant driving but broken up with diverse stops.
  • After several active hiking days, the Mývatn Nature Baths offer a perfect opportunity to relax and recover.
  • Note that despite the midnight sun, some attractions like Goðafoss can be especially beautiful in evening light with fewer tourists.
